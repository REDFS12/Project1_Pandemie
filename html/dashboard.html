About the Viruses

Covid (COVID-19)

Full name: Coronavirus Disease 2019

Causative agent: SARS-CoV-2 (een coronavirus)

Spread: Through droplets in the air (coughing, sneezing, talking)

Symptoms: Fever, cough, fatigue, breathing problems, loss of taste/smell

Prevention: Vaccination, washing hands, wearing a face mask, ventilation

Severity: Can be mild to severe, especially dangerous for the elderly and people with underlying conditions

Seasonal flu

Full name: Influenza

Causative agent: Influenzavirus (usually type A or B)

Spread: Through saliva droplets (coughing, sneezing)

Symptoms: High fever, chills, muscle pain, fatigue, dry cough

Prevention: Annual flu shot, good hygiene

Severity: Usually mild, but can lead to complications in vulnerable groups

Malaria

Causative agent: Plasmodium parasite, transmitted through mosquito bites (Anopheles mosquito)

Spread: Not from person to person humans, only via mosquitoes

Symptoms: Fever, chills, sweating, headache, nausea

Prevention: Mosquito repellents, mosquito nets, antimalarials

Severity: Without treatment it can be fatal, especially in tropical areas
